I am currently a researcher in the Autonomous Systems group at Microsoft. As part of my research, I focus on the development of perception and planning algorithms for robotics, specifically through robust neural networks and the bridging of the simulation-reality gap. I received my PhD from the Texas A&M University, where I was part of the Unmanned Systems Lab headed by Srikanth Saripalli. My PhD thesis focused on collaborative localization and uncertainty-aware navigation for groups of micro aerial vehicles. I also worked on applying computer vision and machine learning to other aerial/ground vehicle research and medical robotics.
Prior to that, I received my MS from the Arizona State University, where I was part of the Extreme Environment Robotics Lab. During these years, I worked on the design and development of various robotic platforms and sensory networks.
